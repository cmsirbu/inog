Talk Submission Guidelines

It can be a 5-10min presentation, PoC (Proof of Concept), or code demo. If you have a traditional 30-40min talk on relevant network related technology, experiences, or previous builds, we'd love to hear from you!

Note: As a general rule, the longer you want to talk/present for, the relatively 'better' you and your content should be i.e. have you given this talk before to a large crowd > 30 (and they asked questions or even laughed along with you?), have you rehearsed and timed it well, have you presented professionally or are aiming to improve your presenting constantly?

Why: Not to put anyone off, we see the short 5-10min talks/presentations as a way for anyone to practice in front of an interested crowd (a really great opportunity to refine a range of presentation skills!) or even to have your first ever crack at standing up and talking about something you are passionate about in front of people.... (a data networking 'toastmasters' as such :)

How: Please email us your topic/idea/talk (and a summary or slides if you have them), tweet us (Cristian or Donal or iNOG), post a message on the Meetup Group, or poke us in Slack.

iNOG Code of Conduct

The short version: Be excellent to each other.

Our group is dedicated to providing a harassment-free event experience for everyone, regardless of gender, gender identity and expression, age, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, or religion (or lack thereof).

We do not tolerate harassment of group participants in any form. Sexual language and imagery is not appropriate for any meetup venue, including talks, workshops, parties, Twitter and other online media. Group participants violating these rules may be sanctioned or expelled from the group (without a refund if applicable) at the discretion of the group organisers.

The long version is at confcodeofconduct.com which is also the source for the above.
