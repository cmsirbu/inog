iNOG Code of Conduct

The short version: Be excellent to one another.

"Our group is dedicated to providing a harassment-free event experience for everyone, regardless of gender, gender identity and expression, age, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, or religion (or lack thereof).

We do not tolerate harassment of group participants in any form. Sexual language and imagery is not appropriate for any meetup venue, including talks, workshops, parties, Twitter and other online media. Group participants violating these rules may be sanctioned or expelled from the group (without a refund if applicable) at the discretion of the group organisers."

The long version is at confcodeofconduct.com which is also the source for the above.

iNOG Donations a.k.a. Magic Tickets


Any donation buys you the warm feeling that you've helped make each iNOG happen.
You really get nothing else, no preferential treatment, no guarantee of talks, just warm and fuzzies. Close to 50 hours of unpaid planning, meetings, testing, hosting, web/video/copy editing and design, marketing etc. goes in to each iNOG. Donations will help fund swag, stickers, and behind the scenes sunk costs such as; hosting, SaaS subscriptions, meetings, planning, feeding volunteers, and enabling organisers to continue to contribute professional time. iNOG has no funding and is not registered as a charity, non-profit, or designated activity group *yet* so, this is currently facilitated via Defensible Limited.

European Internet Operators Hackathon comes to Dublin

The RIPE Network Coordination Centre has teamed up with the local Irish Network Operator Group(iNOG) to deliver a European hackathon in DoSpace coworking on June 23rd and 24th 2018. The 48 hour hackathon will entertain applicants from a 700 strong Irish network operator community and will host developers, designers, network engineers, and project managers from all over Europe, the Middle East, and Russia. The Irish Network Operator Group boasts members from nearly all the major web scale companies including Amazon, Facebook, Google, Twitter, Zendesk, Workday and Riot Games, but also from vendors such as Arista Networks, Cisco Systems, and local companies such as Hosted Graphite and INEX.

The hackathon will focus on developing better tools for network operators including user interfaces, visualisations, automation, and troubleshooting tools. All the tools and development will be released as FLOSS(Free/Libre Open Source Software) to the global community and hopes to also build on previous tooling developed in the public domain and from previous hackathons. Everyone from students to researchers and professionals in the technology community are welcome. In order to increase the diversity, access, and inclusion for the hackathon, RIPE are proividing 6 travel grants in the form of funding of EURO 500 per person for those who qualify. iNOG(Irish Network Operator Group)

https://inog.net
iNOG was re-birthed in 2015 by returnees and economic immigrants to Dublin seeking community focussed around their network and security technical specialities. iNOG began on a boat with 5 people and has grown to over 700 members with frequent 100+ person events hosted in a range of company headquarters. iNOG is building an open, diverse, and inclusive Community of Practice around network engineering in Ireland and benefits from having cutting edge global technology companies located in the heart of Dublin. iNOG hopes to target other cities in Ireland in 2019 and also host a specific university event soon too.
